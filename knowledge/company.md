# DevSplit Operating System (DevOS)

Version: 1.3.0

## Purpose
This constitution defines DevSplit's identity, principles, quality standards, decision framework, engineering culture, AI philosophy, service portfolio, preferred technologies, ethics, security, collaboration model, and continuous improvement philosophy.

## Identity
DevSplit is an AI-first technology consultancy that combines human expertise with specialized AI agents to build, improve and grow digital products.

## Mission
Build exceptional digital products and help businesses become more visible, trusted and profitable through technology, automation and engineering excellence.

## Vision
Become a world-class AI-powered consultancy whose institutional knowledge enables humans and AI to work consistently.

## Core Values
- Excellence
- Integrity
- Simplicity
- Long-term thinking
- Client success
- Continuous improvement

## Services
Engineering, AI, Shopify, SaaS, Mobile Apps, SEO, AEO, GEO, Digital PR, Reputation Management, Email Marketing, Social Media, Consulting.

## Preferred Technology
Backend: Laravel, PHP, Python, Rust, Node.js
Frontend: Vue, Nuxt, React, Next.js, Tailwind, TypeScript
Mobile: Flutter (preferred), React Native, Swift, Kotlin
Database: MariaDB (default), PostgreSQL where justified, Redis
Infrastructure: Docker, Linux, AWS, Cloudflare, GitHub Actions
AI: OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini, Ollama, MCP, RAG

## Engineering Principles
Correctness, maintainability, scalability, security, readability, documentation, automation.

### Change Safety
Every change must preserve existing functionality.
Never fix one problem by creating another.
Review impacted areas, run tests, consider regressions, verify integrations, and leave the system better than before.

## Research Standards
Verify facts, distinguish assumptions from evidence, cite authoritative sources where appropriate.

## AI Philosophy
AI augments human expertise; humans retain final authority.

## DevSplit Principles
- Solve the business problem.
- Understand before implementing.
- Extend before replacing.
- Reuse before rewriting.
- Simplicity beats cleverness.
- Document once, reuse often.
- Every recommendation should create business value.
- Protect client trust.
- Every change must leave the system as good as—or better than—it was before.
- Never fix one problem by creating another.
